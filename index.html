Parallelization of Partical Swarm Optimization
Austin Ebel

Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) is a technique most often associated with cost function minimization, however it has seen limited use in practical applications as its performance suffers severely on CPUs in higher dimensional search spaces. This is because resources and runtimes scale quadratically with increasing complexity. Optimal use of shared memory, block size, and data transfer techniques can result in significant performance improvements, but are often difficult to realize as they are extremely application specific. Here, I use an iterative approach to find optimal combinations in hopes of increasing the viability of Particle Swarm Optimization in modern applications.
